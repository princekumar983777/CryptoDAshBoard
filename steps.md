## Understing the data presentation 
1. Features to Include in Your Crypto Dashboard
Here’s a list of features that make a crypto dashboard useful and impressive:

A. Real-time Data
Fetch cryptocurrency data every few seconds (e.g., 10 sec).
Track price, market cap, volume, circulating supply, price change % (1h, 24h, 7d).
Show top gainers and losers.

B. Historical Data & Trends
Store data in a database for historical analysis.
Show price trends via line charts, candlestick charts, moving averages.
Allow filtering by time range: 1h, 24h, 7d, 1M, 1Y.

C. Portfolio Simulation
Allow users to track their mock portfolio.
Show profit/loss, allocation charts (pie chart), and performance graphs.

D. Market Analytics
Top 10 coins by market cap
Volume leaders
Price volatility indicators
Correlation heatmap (optional advanced feature)

E. Alerts & Notification
Price alerts (when a coin crosses a threshold)
News feed integration (optional)

F. Data Filtering & Interactivity
Filters for exchange, category, gainers/losers, etc.
Interactive charts and tables.

G. Visualization Ideas
Line chart → price trend
Candlestick chart → open/high/low/close.
Bar chart → trading volumes.
Pie chart → portfolio allocation.
Heatmap → correlation between coins.
